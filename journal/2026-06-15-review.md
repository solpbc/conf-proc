## Review path (suggested order)

1. **`lib/hcl.sh`** — the substance. Check in particular:
   - `hcl_runtime_json`: extracts the JSON by anchoring on the first `{"` at/after
     offset 1216 and reading to the first NUL. **This is the riskiest assumption**
     — it sidesteps needing to know the exact variable-data metadata length, but
     it only holds if the JSON has no embedded NUL (true) and Azure NUL-pads the
     NV region (expected). The binding check in step 6 is what confirms we got the
     byte range exactly right.
   - `hcl_verify_ak_binding`: proves the AK by matching RSA moduli rather than
     reconstructing a PEM from the JWK. Worth a sanity check that you're happy
     with that approach (the quote is still verified under the TPM's own AK PEM).
   - `hcl_binding_hash`: order/exactness of `domain ∥ nonce ∥ guest_pubkey ∥ ctx`.
2. **`run.sh` steps 5–8** — orchestration: `tpm2_nvread`, the binding checks,
   `tpm2_readpublic`, `tpm2_quote`/`tpm2_checkquote`. Note the env knobs
   (`PCR_LIST`, `NONCE_HEX`, `AK_HANDLE`, `HCL_NV_INDEX`, `BINDING_DOMAIN`, `CTX_FILE`).
3. **`test/freshness-selftest.sh`** — see how the fixture is built and convince
   yourself the positive/negative cases actually exercise the logic.
4. **`README.md`** "What `run.sh` implements" — confirms the narrative matches.

  auth on this image.
